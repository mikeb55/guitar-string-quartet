# Listening References

Practical lessons from Bill Frisell’s string-oriented recordings.

## Richter 858

- **Wide spacing** — Strings occupy distinct registers; avoid crowding.
- **Sustained tones** — Long notes, slow decay, minimal articulation.
- **Transparent orchestration** — Every note audible; no doubling for thickness.
- **Chamber dialogue** — Instruments respond to each other rather than playing in lockstep.
- **Use of silence** — Gaps between phrases; breath before re-entry.
- **Emotional restraint** — Intensity through control, not volume.

## Sign of Life

- **Guitar integration** — Guitar weaves in and out of string texture; not always on top.
- **Modal colour shifts** — Harmony moves slowly; melody drives changes.
- **Inner voice motion** — Viola and Violin II carry harmonic and contrapuntal weight.
- **Cello as anchor** — Pedal points and bass motion when needed; otherwise sparse.

## When You Wish Upon a Star

- **Lyrical simplicity** — Melody first; harmony supports.
- **Sparse accompaniment** — Not every instrument every bar.
- **Conversational phrasing** — Call and response between guitar and strings.
- **Restraint** — Understatement over grand gesture.

## Big Sur

- **Landscape quality** — Spacious, unhurried development.
- **Independent voices** — Each part has its own line; minimal block harmony.
- **Slow harmonic bloom** — Changes unfold over long spans.
- **Texture variation** — Contrast between full ensemble and subsets (e.g. guitar + viola, dark trio).

## Extracted Principles

| Principle | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| Wide spacing | Keep string registers distinct; avoid tutti in same octave |
| Sustained tones | Prefer long notes over busy passagework |
| Transparent orchestration | One note per part when possible; no redundant doubling |
| Chamber dialogue | Write response, not accompaniment |
| Silence | Include rests; allow phrases to breathe |
| Restraint | Dynamics and intensity through control, not density |
