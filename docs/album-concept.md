# Album Concept

## Creative Phase

This album represents the follow-up creative phase after Mike Bryant’s first recording session with the Sylva Quartet. The music builds on that collaboration and extends into a new body of work written specifically for guitar and string quartet.

## Genre and Identity

Jazz-influenced chamber writing. The quartet functions as a fluid chamber ensemble rather than a classical block. Guitar is the central voice but not always the lead; the strings have independent melodic and textural roles.

## Core Ideas

- **Guitar as central voice** — Primary melodic and harmonic protagonist, but integrated into the ensemble rather than dominating.
- **Quartet as chamber ensemble** — Responsive, conversational, capable of sparse and dense textures. Not a backing section.
- **Chamber jazz** — ECM-influenced, spacious, modal colour, lyrical phrasing, restraint.
- **Collaboration** — Written for the Sylva Quartet; idiomatic, expressive, and rehearsal-ready.

## What This Album Is Not

- A classical string quartet with guitar accompaniment
- Cinematic underscore or generic string pads
- Dense, constant tutti writing
- Symmetrical or formulaic phrasing
