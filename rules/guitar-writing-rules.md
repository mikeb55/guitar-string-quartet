# Guitar Writing Rules

## Guitar-First Priority

In this repository, the guitar is a compositional driver.

The piece should often be thinkable as "guitar harmony + string response," not "strings plus incidental guitar."

The guitar part must create identity, pacing, and harmonic colour from the outset.

**See `rules/guitar-first-composition-rule.md` for full specification.**

---

## Guitar Harmonic Requirement

The guitar must not default to monophonic writing.

### Minimum Distribution

- **40–60%** dyads or double-stops
- **20–30%** triads or chord fragments
- **10–20%** single-note melodic material

### Required Guitar Textures

- dyadic counterlines vs violin
- triadic punctuations
- quartal voicings
- melody + interval double-stop
- chord hits aligned with ensemble accents
- occasional sustained harmonic colours

### Avoid

- extended single-note passages longer than 4 bars
- guitar acting only as lead melody

### Guitar Functions

The guitar must function as:

- melodic voice
- harmonic colour
- counterline source
- rhythmic punctuation

---

## Polyphonic Default

The guitar must not default to monophonic writing.

## Harmonic Content

Future guitar parts must regularly include:

- Dyads
- Double-stops
- Triads
- Partial voicings
- Small chord fragments

## Minimum Polyphonic Proportion

At least **40 percent** of guitar material in generated pieces should involve more than one pitch.

## Texture Types to Include

- Melody + dyad textures
- Triadic punctuations
- Harmonic hits with the strings
- Quartal fragments
- Occasional sustained chord colours

Apply these rules in combination with `instrumentation-rules.md` and `quartet-texture-rules.md`.
