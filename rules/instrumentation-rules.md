# Instrumentation Rules

Roles for each instrument in the guitar + string quartet ensemble.

## Guitar

- **Primary:** Melodic lead, harmonic colour, countervoice
- **Range:** Idiomatic; avoid extreme tessitura
- **Texture:** Can carry melody alone; can drop out for string-only sections
- **Integration:** Weaves with strings; not always on top; capable of interior harmony
- **Avoid:** Constant strumming; dominating every bar; doubling string melody in octaves

**See also:** `guitar-writing-rules.md` — guitar must not default to monophonic writing; ≥40% polyphonic (dyads, triads, chord fragments).

## Violin I

- **Primary:** Light-bearing melodic line
- **Role:** High register; lyrical; often carries primary melody when guitar rests
- **Texture:** Can float above ensemble; can join inner voices when appropriate
- **Avoid:** Constant high sustained notes; generic descant

## Violin II

- **Primary:** Interior motion, harmonic bridge
- **Role:** Connects Violin I and Viola; harmonic filler; contrapuntal line
- **Texture:** Often in mid register; supports without doubling
- **Avoid:** Mechanical thirds/sixths with Violin I; redundant doubling

## Viola

- **Primary:** Glue voice, harmonic destabiliser
- **Role:** Bridges violin and cello registers; adds colour; can destabilise harmony with suspensions or upper structures
- **Texture:** Interior; often carries harmonic tension
- **Avoid:** Constant block harmony; invisible filler

## Cello

- **Primary:** Anchor, pedal source, lyrical baritone
- **Role:** Bass when needed; pedal points; can carry melody in baritone register
- **Texture:** Foundation or lyrical; not always active
- **Avoid:** Constant quarter-note bass; every-bar activity

## Ensemble Principles

- **Subset writing:** 2–3 instruments often; full quintet is an event
- **Register separation:** Keep parts in distinct ranges when tutti
- **Independence:** Each part has its own logic; avoid lockstep
