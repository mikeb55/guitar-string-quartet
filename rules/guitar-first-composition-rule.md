# Guitar-First Composition Rule

In this repository, the guitar must be treated as a primary compositional engine, not an afterthought.

## Default Generation Order

1. Generate guitar harmonic vocabulary first
2. Generate guitar rhythmic identity second
3. Generate guitar melodic / dyadic material third
4. Build string counterlines and textures around the guitar
5. Only then refine full ensemble interaction

## Core Principle

The guitar part must contain a meaningful harmonic identity before the strings are written.

The string quartet must respond to, frame, contrast with, or intensify the guitar material.

The guitar must never default to an extended monophonic lead line unless explicitly requested.

---

## Guitar Material Requirements

Before a composition is considered valid, the guitar must include all of the following:

- at least 3 dyadic passages
- at least 3 triadic or chord-fragment passages
- at least 1 section where guitar harmony drives the ensemble texture
- at least 1 passage where the guitar interacts contrapuntally with violin
- at least 1 passage where the guitar contributes rhythmic chord hits or punctuations

## Target Distribution

Default guitar texture balance:

- **35–45%** dyads / double-stops
- **25–35%** triads / chord fragments / small voicings
- **10–20%** sustained colour chords
- **10–20%** single-note melodic material

Single-note writing must not dominate the guitar part.

---

## Preferred Guitar Textures

Use combinations of:

- melody + dyad
- open triads
- suspended dyads
- quartal fragments
- triad pairs
- upper-structure fragments
- contrary-motion double-stops
- chord punctuations with space
- slow harmonic tremolo or repeated colour voicings where musically justified

---

## Disallowed Defaults

Avoid:

- long stretches of monophonic single-note guitar
- static block comping
- empty guitar bars while strings do all the harmonic work
- chord symbols that contradict actual guitar pitches
- repetitive 4-bar loop guitar writing
- mechanically repeated chord hits without rhythmic evolution

---

## String Relationship Rule

Once the guitar material exists, the strings must be generated in relation to it.

String functions should include:

- answering guitar dyads
- shadowing guitar rhythm at contrast points
- providing contrary motion against guitar chords
- sustaining tones that enrich guitar voicings
- creating ensemble hits around guitar chord attacks

At least one section per piece must feature explicit guitar–violin dialogue.

At least one section per piece must feature guitar harmony supported by viola/cello interior motion.

---

## Quality Control Rule

If the guitar part is still mostly monophonic, the composition fails quality control and must be regenerated.

If the guitar does not clearly shape the harmony and identity of the piece, the composition fails quality control and must be regenerated.
