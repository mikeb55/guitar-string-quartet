# Anti-Monotony Composition Rule

## Purpose

Prevent static, repetitive textures and ensure evolving musical material across all generated compositions.

## Repetition Fatigue Threshold

A motif or harmonic pattern may repeat:

- **Maximum 2 times** identically
- **Maximum 3 times** with variation

After that, at least one of the following must change:

- rhythm
- harmony
- orchestration
- register
- counterline
- density
- articulation

## Static Texture Limit

No texture (same rhythmic pattern + same instrumentation role) may remain unchanged longer than:

**6 bars**

After 6 bars the engine must introduce at least one of:

- new counterline
- rhythmic variation
- harmonic colour shift
- ensemble hit
- register shift
- instrumentation role change

## Guitar Repetition Limit

The guitar may not repeat the same chord gesture more than twice without variation.

Variation methods include:

- dyad inversion
- altered rhythm
- chord extension
- added melodic fragment
- interaction with violin or viola

## String Motion Rule

If two or more string parts sustain the same rhythmic texture for more than 6 bars:

- introduce inner motion
- shift register
- create counterpoint
- change articulation

## Formal Evolution Rule

Every composition must demonstrate at least three structural phases:

1. introduction or texture establishment
2. development or density growth
3. transformation or release

Pieces that remain texturally static fail validation.

## Quality Control

If repetition fatigue or static texture is detected, regenerate material until variation is introduced.
