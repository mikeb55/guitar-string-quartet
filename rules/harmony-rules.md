# Harmony Rules

Harmonic language for this project.

## Melody-Driven Harmony

Harmony follows melody. Chord choices support the melodic contour; avoid harmony that dictates melody.

## Modal Colour Shifts

- Use modal interchange (e.g. bVII, IVm)
- Lydian, Dorian, Mixolydian as primary colours
- Major/minor ambiguity when appropriate

## Upper-Structure Thinking

- Extensions (9, 11, 13) as melody notes
- Chords can be implied by upper voices
- Non-root bass when appropriate

## Non-Root Support Tones

- Suspensions (4–3, 7–6, 9–8)
- Pedal points against changing harmony
- Passing tones and neighbour notes in harmony

## Suspension Chains

- Sequential suspensions for tension
- Delayed resolution
- Voice-leading over root movement

## Planing When Appropriate

- Parallel chord motion for colour
- Use sparingly; not as default
- Justify with melodic or textural intent

## Avoid

- Formulaic I–IV–V–I without melodic justification
- Constant root-position triads
- Generic cinematic progressions (e.g. endless iv–I)
- Harmony that overrides melodic identity
