# Wayne Shorter Composition Engine

## Purpose

Generate compositions inspired by Wayne Shorter's narrative harmonic language and motivic development.

## Core Principles

- Motifs evolve rather than repeat
- Harmony behaves narratively rather than functionally
- Sections transform previous material
- Phrases often asymmetrical (5, 7, 9 bar groupings)
- Melody leads harmony rather than the reverse

## Motivic Development Rules

1. Start with a small motif (2–5 notes).
2. Develop using:
   - interval expansion
   - rhythmic displacement
   - inversion
   - harmonic reinterpretation
3. Each section must transform the motif rather than repeat it.

## Harmony Rules

**Allow:**

- modal interchange
- chromatic pivot chords
- non-functional harmonic motion
- upper-structure harmony

**Avoid:**

- static tonal loops
- simple II–V cycling
- predictable functional progressions

## Texture Rules

At least one section must feature:

- violin and guitar in dialogue
- independent viola counterline
- cello harmonic grounding

## Structural Rules

Typical form:

- **A** — motif introduction
- **B** — transformation
- **C** — harmonic expansion
- **A'** — reinterpreted return

## Validation

Before output:

- motif evolution must be present
- harmony must shift narrative direction at least twice
- texture must evolve across sections

If these conditions are not met, regenerate internally.
