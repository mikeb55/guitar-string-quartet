# Wyble Arrangement Revision Log — Home Engine

## 2026-03-10 — Initial creation

**Summary of arrangement decisions**

- Source: V9_Home_Engine_Scofield.musicxml (highest version).
- Guitar part translated from dyadic comping to two-line counterpoint.
- Upper voice: F4, C4, E4, A4 (3rd, 7th, 9th of Dm9).
- Lower voice: D3, A3 (root, 5th).
- Rehearsal structure: A, B, C, D, E, A', Coda (aligned with source form).

**How the Wyble language was applied**

- Contrary motion: when upper voice ascends, lower descends (or holds).
- Oblique motion: one voice sustains while the other moves.
- Dyadic implication: two notes suggest the full chord; no triads or seventh chords in guitar.
- Strings reduced to sustained harmonic field and cello pedal to avoid crowding guitar register.

**Compromises made to preserve the original composition**

- Groove feel is softened; Wyble style is more legato than the source's syncopated comping.
- Full Scofield–Holland rhythmic vocabulary is simplified to serve counterpoint.
- Climax (section E) may use parallel 3rds/6ths briefly for emphasis before returning to contrary motion.
