# Notes — working-title-01

## Initial Idea

Contemplative opening: guitar and viola in close register, cello holding a low D. No violins for the first section. The piece should feel like a slow exhale — restraint, space, melodic fragments that don't resolve immediately.

## Motivic Seed Ideas

**Primary motif:** Descending minor-third cell (D–B–C#) — three notes, lyrical, can be stretched across bars. The C# (Lydian #4) gives colour; B suggests Dorian shift.

**Response motif:** Rising fourth (A–D) — answer to the descent; appears in viola or violin II as inner voice before guitar takes it.

**Rhythmic cell:** Long note — short rest — two shorter notes. Asymmetrical; avoids beat 1 emphasis. Phrase can spill over bar line (e.g. start on beat 3 of bar 2, resolve on beat 2 of bar 4).

## Harmonic Sketch

- **A:** D Lydian stasis; cello pedal D; guitar and viola move in upper structure (9, 11, #11).
- **B:** Shift to B Dorian; inner voices (Violin II, Viola) carry harmonic motion; guitar enters late with lyrical elaboration. Suspension chain: Bm7 — Em7 — A(add9) — Dmaj7.
- **A':** Return to D; full ensemble briefly in split register bloom; thins to guitar + cello for closing gesture. Final chord: Dmaj7(#11) with guitar on high D, strings fading.

## Development Notes

- Section lengths deliberately asymmetrical (7, 9, 5) to avoid 4+4 feel.
- Guitar never dominates; when it carries melody, strings provide sparse support or rest.
- Improvisation space: B section, bars 5–7 — guitar can elaborate over inner-voice weave; written melody as skeleton.
- Silence: 2-bar rest after A before B; 1-bar rest before A' re-entry.
