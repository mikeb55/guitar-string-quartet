# Arrangement Plan — working-title-01

## Form Overview

| Section | Bars | Texture | Key Area |
|---------|------|---------|----------|
| A | 1–7 | Cello pedal foundation + Guitar + Viola duet | D Lydian |
| B | 8–16 | Inner voice weave; guitar enters bar 4 | B Dorian |
| A' | 17–21 | Split register bloom → Guitar + Cello counterpoint | D Lydian |

## Texture Choices by Section

### Section A (bars 1–7)
- **Texture model:** Cello pedal foundation + Guitar + Viola duet
- **Active instruments:** Cello, Guitar, Viola
- **Role per instrument:**
  - Guitar: Lyrical melody; primary motif (D–B–C#); mid register
  - Violin I: tacet
  - Violin II: tacet
  - Viola: Response motif (A–D); conversational with guitar
  - Cello: Pedal D (whole notes or half notes); anchor

### Section B (bars 8–16)
- **Texture model:** Inner voice weave → guitar joins
- **Active instruments:** Violin II, Viola (bars 8–11); Guitar enters bar 12; optional Violin I shimmer bars 14–16
- **Role per instrument:**
  - Guitar: Enters bar 12 with developed motif; improvisation space bars 14–16
  - Violin I: tacet until bar 14 (optional sustained high B or E)
  - Violin II: Contrapuntal inner line; harmonic motion
  - Viola: Contrapuntal inner line; suspension chain
  - Cello: Sparse; pedal B or stepwise motion; not constant

### Section A' (bars 17–21)
- **Texture model:** Split register bloom (bars 17–18) → Guitar + Cello counterpoint (bars 19–21)
- **Active instruments:** All five briefly; then Guitar + Cello
- **Role per instrument:**
  - Guitar: Primary motif; high register; final gesture alone
  - Violin I: Shimmer layer (sustained high D); exits bar 19
  - Violin II: Brief interior motion; exits bar 18
  - Viola: Brief interior motion; exits bar 18
  - Cello: Anchor; then counterpoint with guitar; fades before guitar's final note

## Entry and Exit Points

- **First entry:** Cello (bar 1), Guitar (bar 1), Viola (bar 2)
- **Full ensemble:** Bars 17–18 only (split register bloom)
- **Sparse moments:** A entirely (3 instruments); B bars 8–11 (2 instruments); A' bars 19–21 (2 then 1)
- **Final gesture:** Guitar alone, high D, fermata; strings have rested

## Dynamic Arc

- **Opening:** mp — intimate, restrained
- **B section:** mf at guitar entry (bar 12); subsides to mp by bar 16
- **A' climax:** mf at full ensemble (bar 17); diminuendo through bar 21
- **Closing:** p — guitar final note; silence after

## Ensemble Interaction Ideas

- **Call and response:** Guitar motif (A bars 1–3) answered by Viola (bars 4–5)
- **Chamber dialogue:** B section — Violin II and Viola converse; guitar enters as third voice
- **Improvisation space:** B bars 14–16 — guitar elaborates; written melody as skeleton; strings provide sparse support or sustain

## Possible Improvisation Space for Guitar

**Location:** B section, bars 14–16 (approx. bars 5–7 of B)

**Context:** Inner voices (Violin II, Viola) sustain or provide sparse harmonic hits. Chord area: Bm7 — Em7 — A(add9).

**Approach:** Written melody (D–B–C#–E–D arc) as skeleton. Guitar may elaborate rhythmically, add passing tones, extend phrase. Keep lyrical; avoid virtuosic runs. Restraint over density.

**Duration:** ~3 bars; return to written material for transition into A'.
